Our Story

Three Generations,
One Hollow Creek

🌿

Hollow Creek Farm began in 1962 when Bill and Margaret Calloway purchased 40 acres of rolling Virginia land with little more than a handshake, a tractor, and a shared dream of building something that would last. What started as a modest produce stand along the roadside grew — harvest by harvest — into the beloved seasonal destination it is today.

Their children grew up working the fields, learning the rhythms of the land before they learned to drive. When Bill and Margaret passed the farm to the next generation, their son Rob and his wife Dana didn't just inherit a property — they inherited a way of life. They expanded the farm store, launched the first U-Pick sunflower season, and introduced the farm-to-table dinners that guests now book months in advance.

Today, Rob and Dana's children — Tyler, Meg, and Sam — are carrying the farm into its third generation. They've brought fresh energy to the events calendar, the online shop, and the seasonal experiences, while making sure the heart of what Bill and Margaret built is never lost.

"We don't just grow food here. We grow memories." — Dana Calloway
